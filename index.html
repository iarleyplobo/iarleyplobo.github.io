Theoretical Physics · Quantum Gravity Phenomenology

Iarley Pereira Lobo

Professor at the Department of Chemistry and Physics, Center for Agricultural Sciences, Federal University of Paraíba. My research explores phenomenological signatures of quantum gravity, modified/deformed relativistic symmetries, non-Riemannian geometries, cosmic rays, black-hole perturbations, and gravitational-wave phenomenology.

I am also a member of the Virgo Collaboration, contributing to the detection and analysis of gravitational waves.

Research themes

Quantum gravity phenomenology, Lorentz invariance violation, deformed special relativity, Finsler/Hamilton geometry, ultra-high-energy cosmic rays, black-hole quasinormal modes, and tabletop/infrared tests of quantum spacetime.

Current projects

Probing quantum gravity through deviations from Riemannian geometry, with applications to cosmic-ray propagation, atmospheric showers, gravitational waves, and modified Schrödinger dynamics.

Teaching

General Physics, Mechanics, Fluid Mechanics, and introductory physics courses for Agronomy, Chemistry, and Biology students.
