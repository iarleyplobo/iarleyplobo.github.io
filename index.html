Theoretical Physics · Quantum Gravity Phenomenology

Iarley Pereira Lobo

Professor at the Physics Department, Federal University of Paraíba. My research explores phenomenological signatures of quantum gravity, modified/deformed relativistic symmetries, non-Riemannian geometries, cosmic rays, black-hole perturbations, and gravitational-wave phenomenology.

I am also a member of the Virgo Collaboration, through the VirgoBR group, contributing to the detection and analysis of gravitational waves.

Research themes

Quantum gravity phenomenology, Lorentz invariance violation, deformed special relativity, Finsler geometry, ultra-high-energy cosmic rays, gravitational waves and tabletop/infrared tests of quantum spacetime.

Current projects

Probing quantum gravity through deviations from Riemannian geometry, with applications to cosmic-ray propagation, atmospheric showers, gravitational waves, and modified Schrödinger dynamics.
