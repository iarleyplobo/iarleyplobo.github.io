Research

My research focuses on possible phenomenological traces of quantum gravity and on geometric frameworks beyond standard Riemannian geometry.

Quantum gravity phenomenology

I investigate modified dispersion relations, Lorentz invariance violation, deformed relativistic symmetries, κ-Poincaré/κ-(A)dS structures, and their implications for high-energy astrophysics and precision experiments.

Cosmic rays and astroparticles

Current work includes simulations of atmospheric showers, modified lifetimes of unstable particles, propagation of ultra-high-energy cosmic rays, and Planck-scale effects in particle thresholds and acceleration mechanisms.

Black holes and gravitational waves

I study black-hole perturbations, quasinormal modes, and potential signatures of non-standard gravitational physics in gravitational-wave observations. I am a member of the Virgo Collaboration.

Infrared and tabletop probes

I also explore modified Schrödinger dynamics, Lindblad-type effects, quantum correlations, and possible low-energy manifestations of quantum-spacetime structure.
